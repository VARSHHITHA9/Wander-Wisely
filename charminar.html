CHARMINAR

Overview

Charminar has become an iconic symbol of Hyderabad. This 16th-century monument was constructed by the Qutb Shahi dynasty, to commemorate the founding of Hyderabad and to combat a deadly epidemic spreading in the Golconda region.

The four towering minarets, dominating the Hyderabad skyline, serve as timeless reminders of the architectural prowess of that era. Additionally, Charminar houses a mosque and prayer rooms.

Charminar holds historical and religious significance and is renowned for its bustling local markets surrounding the structure, making it one of the most visited tourist attractions in Hyderabad.

  • Location : Char Kaman, Ghansi Bazaar, Hyderabad, Telangana.
  • Entry Fee : INR 5/- per person.
