Mecca Masjid

Overview

One of the oldest and the largest mosque in India, Mecca Masjid is made of soil and bricks exported from Mecca in 1694. The 75 feet high mosque has the capacity to accommodate 10,000 people at one time and takes the name from the Grand Mosque of Mecca.

Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah played a pivotal role in the construction of this mosque. It is said that around 8000 masons worked round the clock to complete the construction in 77 years. The three fascinating and exquisitely designed facades are carved out of a single granite stone which took the large team of workers five years to quarry.

This nearby attraction is a must-visit when exploring Charminar, offering a complementary experience that enhances your visit.

  • Location : Laad Bazaar Rd, Khilwat, Hyderabad.
  • Entry Fee : no entry fee.
